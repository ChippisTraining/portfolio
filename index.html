HTML & CSS—Nice and Simple. Or So They Say.

HTML? Fine. Nice and simple. It’s just boxes inside boxes, easy peasy. CSS? Now that’s a different story. CSS is a wee bit of a show-off, always trying to be fancy. “Look at me, I can do gradients! Ohh, I can float things!” Yeah? Well, float this: Half the time, you tell CSS to do something, and it just laughs in your face.

— The 9th Doctor

JavaScript? Ohhh, JavaScript is Mad.

HTML and CSS? Pffft. Simple stuff. But JavaScript? JavaScript is like trying to have a conversation with a hyperactive squirrel. You tell it one thing, it does another. You try to keep it contained, and suddenly, it's off in the distance, throwing errors you’ve never even heard of!
You blink, and there’s a floating div. You blink again, and your entire layout’s gone. One minute, everything works. The next, boom—undefined. Undefined! What does that even mean?!

— The 10th Doctor

Oi, JavaScript’s Brilliant! If You Know What You’re Doing.

Alright, alright! Yes, JavaScript’s a bit... unpredictable. But that’s what makes it fun! It’s like a good timey-wimey paradox—you don’t fight it, you go with it. Embrace the chaos, Ten! It’s just a matter of knowing how to talk to it.
Bit of a conversation, really. You say "please," it says "maybe." You threaten it, and suddenly it listens. And if it doesn't? Well, that’s why we have console logs.

— The 11th Doctor

I Am Talking to It! It’s Just Laughing at Me!

Oh yeah, because JavaScript totally respects politeness. "Oh, please work, kind sir!" And what does it do? Throws an error anyway! “Unexpected token this.” Unexpected?! Unexpected for WHO? I literally just wrote it, how is it unexpected?
And don’t even get me started on asynchronous nonsense! "Oh, let’s wait for this thing." "Oops, too late! It’s already broken!" Absolute nightmare.

— The 10th Doctor

Oh, Stop Whining! It’s Called "Asynchronous" For a Reason!

It’s not magic, Ten! It’s a feature! JavaScript doesn't wait around, it gets on with it. You just need to know how to handle it! Promises, async/await—use them properly and it all makes sense.

— The 11th Doctor

Och, Would Ye All Just Shut Up? Python’s Better.

Listen to yourselves! "Ohh, JavaScript’s chaotic!" "Ohh, HTML is a mess!" Ye know what’s not a mess? Python. Python just works. Ye tell it something, and it does it. None of this asynchronous nonsense—just nice, readable code.
One line, job done. No curly brackets, no semicolons, no crying into your tea because JavaScript decided it didn’t like your syntax today. Aye, simple.

— The 12th Doctor

Oi, All of You—Shut It!

Honestly, you lot! Fighting about programming languages like it’s some kind of galactic war. Get a grip! They all have their uses. JavaScript’s weird but powerful. Python’s easy but slow. HTML and CSS? Essential.
So! How about we stop bickering and just get back to writing some code, yeah?
